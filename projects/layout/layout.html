Seongsan Ilchulbong

Seongsan Ilchulbong was once a small volcanic island located 1 kilometer away from Jeju. Later, accumulated volcanic ash formed a narrow path connecting the island to Jeju, which was visible only at low tide. Nowadays, you can use a road to get there without waiting for the tide.

Since it is located in the far east, many people go there to watch the sunrise, and the name "Ilchul" comes from the word "sunrise." I didn't get to catch the sunrise but the scenery at the top was worth it. For those who plan to hike there, DO NOT CLIMB TOO FAST! Although it is a small mountain, my legs were burning for the rest of the trip in Jeju.
